\texttt{commonInterestScore} is defined as follows:

  such that the \emph{Post} has no \emph{Tag} that the start

\item
  \texttt{commonInterestScore} = \texttt{common} - \texttt{uncommon}

	relevance &
		\footnotesize This query looks for paths of length two, starting from a \emph{Person}
and ending at the friends of their friends. It does widely scattered
graph traversal, and one expects no locality of in friends of friends,
as these have been acquired over a long time and have widely scattered
identifiers. The join order is simple but one must see that the
anti-join for ``not in my friends'' is better with hash. Also the last
pattern in the scalar sub-queries joining or anti-joining the
\emph{Tags} of the candidate's \emph{Posts} to interests of self should
be by hash.
